Fort San Felipe

Fort San Felipe is a military fortress in Cavite City, in the Province of Cavite, Philippines. It was constructed by the Spanish military in 1609 in the first port town of Cavite, the historic core of the present and larger Cavite City, for its protection. Less than half of the original historic structure survives today. The remaining structure is made of granite blocks with 30-foot high walls and features a wide stairway leading to the top of the bastions and remaining walls. Naval memorabilia including antique cannons and cannonballs decorate the lawns of the fortification. Fort San Felipe is located within the 9-hectare (22-acre) Naval Base Cavite of the Philippine Navy and is not open to the public.

Julian Felipe Monument

Did you know that Julian Felipe was born on this very land of ours? Yes, you can even search it up! A commemorative bust was built for him to honor his achievements such as our national anthem “Lupang Hinirang” and being one of the heroes from the past.

Ladislao Diwa Shrine

Visit Don Ladislao Diwa! He is the co-founder of the KKK (Kataastaasang, Kagalanggalangan, Katipunan) and an unsung hero. This is his ancestral home and his remains was burried on the actual grounds of the Shrine.

Corregidor

An island with scars of the second world war. This war memorial and national monument is a frightening yet amazing place to visit. In this place, you can view the artillery and different buildings, tunnels and strategic hideouts where the scars of the past can be seen.

Samonte Park

Muralla is the spot to be when you just want to relax, eat some streets foods and chill with your friends, all while watching the beautiful sunset that awaits.

San Roque Church

San Roque Parish Church, simply known in Cavite City as "San Roque", is a church that was built more than 300 years ago. Although there have been numerous renovations, the stories within the windows and the beauty of the church will still give you wonders!

Food!

Pansit Pusitmore_vert

Available at Bernie's Kitchenette

Pansit Pusitclose

Want to try some exotic food unique only to Cavite City? Eat some of our Pansit Pusit. This noodle dish is unlike your well-known pancit bihon, instead of pork or chicken, we use squid and its ink, that is why it is black. Fear not of the food’s color but the chance you will miss if you don’t give this a try!

Bacalaomore_vert

Available at Bernie's Kitchenette

Bacalaoclose

What about some historically inherited food? Bacalao is a popular salted cod fish dish that we inherited from the Portuguese from the Spanish occupation. This food is present in every Lenten season, especially on Good Friday. A dish worthy of your taste buds!

You found yourself short on cash, what do you do? Eat in our very own Aling Ika’s Carinderia Dishes. Not only do you feel the Caviteño’s love for food but also deliver you with dishes that are within your budget and superb in taste. But don’t forget to taste their special Bibingkoy, words cannot describe their dish. Let your taste buds explain! (P.S. Pair it with “Ginataan” and you will feel heaven!)

Tradition and History

Fiesta of the Porta Vaga

more_vert

Learn more about the City's Reyna!

Fiesta of the Porta Vagaclose

Every second Sunday of November, we, Caviteños, celebrate a special festival for Our Lady of Porta Vaga. It is without a doubt that we are religious and no wonder that in almost every home, altars and doorsteps are not complete without an image of the Soledad. This just implies the myriad of miracles and blessings that the Soledad have given us. Join our religious festival to celebrate the “Queen of Cavite”.

Regada Festival

more_vert

Learn more about the annual water festival!

Regada Festivalclose

“Bawal ang pikon” is the saying of every caviteño once June 24 of every year comes. It basically means that you don’t have the right to be angry. “Why”, you ask? Because in every street, corner or house in cavite city, you will get splashed by water! This traditional event is our way to celebrate in honor of the feast of our Patron, Saint John the Baptist. (Pro Tip: Bring a WATER GUN!)

Navidad Festival

more_vert

From bangenges to parols, here is what in store for you when you join the navidad festival!

Navidad Festivalclose

Let’s watch the creativity from Cavite City! Navidad is a tradition around the second week of December. Here you will witness the different entries of different schools for the Pasiklaban ng mga Bangenge (gigantic paper maches competition), Parada ng mga Kuneho(Rabbit Parade) and Lantern Making Contest. All of these majestic competitions display the creativity of all ages!
