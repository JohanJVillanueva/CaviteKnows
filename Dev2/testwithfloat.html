Muralla – A place where you can take a breath

Muralla is the go to places of all citizens of Cavite City especially in the night. In here, you can eat variety of foods such as barbeques, the famous “tusok-tusok”, as well as your average rice meal. They also provide different refreshments like sodas, juice, shakes, and, of course, milktea. Since it is a place where it does not have a single theme, its best to try every single booth to savor the experience just like the saying “the more the merrier”

Cavite National High School

Cavite National High School (CNHS) is located at Cavite City in the Philippines. It was established in June 19, 1902 and it is considered as one of the oldest national secondary schools in the country. With the ever growing world of technology, it certainly does not fall behind but that doesn’t mean the school is being changed. The design of the library building as well as its right and left wing was specifically modified to stay the same to honor its age. And that’s why it is one of the most treasured places in Cavite City.

Samonte Park

One place that is being adored by a lot of people in the city is their Samonte Park. The best time for you to visit and check out the place is during the night. In the morning you can see a lot of people taking a walk in the place and enjoy the beauty of nature but during the night, everything will be even more exciting. There would be lights in each stop of the place and you could see a lot more vendors that are just starting to set up their products along the specified sidewalk area. During the night, the true life in Samonte Park shines better.
Photo credits to Allan Bella

Ladislao Diwa Shrine

Ladislao Diwa y Nocon (June 27, 1863 − March 12, 1930) was a Filipino patriot who was among the founders of the Katipunan that initiated the Philippine Revolution against Spain in 1896. Specifically, Don Ladislao Diwa is well remembered as the co-founder of the KKK (Kataastaasang, Kagalanggalangan, Katipunan). The National Historical Institute(NHI) declared his ancestral home as a national shrine. On November 30, l996, his mortal remains were transferred to the Ladislao Diwa Mausoleum on the grounds of the Shrine. Credits to Cavite.gov.ph

Corregidor

Corregidor Island is an island located at the entrance of Manila Bay in southwestern part of Luzon Island in the Philippines. Due to this location, Corregidor has historically been fortified with coastal artillery to defend the entrance of Manila Bay and Manila from attacks by enemy warships. Corregidor (Fort Mills) is the largest of the islands that formed the harbor defenses of Manila Bay, together with El Fraile Island (Fort Drum), Caballo Island (Fort Hughes) and Carabao Island (Fort Frank), which were all fortified during the American colonial period. The island was also the site of a small military airfield, as part of the defense.

One of the most favorite delicacies of Cavite City and bread filling by pure blooded Caviteno. Made of ground peanuts and galapong that is steamed and wrapped in banana leaves. It is one of the most famous and highly favorite dish best eaten and paired with Adobo and Kare-Kare during Sundays. Photo from LutongCavite.Blogspot.com

Bibingkoy is the classic Chabacano adaptation of the tang yuan. Patties of galapong are prepared and filled with sweetened red mongo bean paste. The patties are lightly arranged in baking pan and cooked in the manner of bibingka. It is served with thick soup made from gata (coconut cream), with added pieces of camote, langka, bilo-bilo, flavored with pandan and sweetened with panocha. Photo from lutongcavite.blogspot.com
